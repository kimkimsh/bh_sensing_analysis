# Beyond Honeycomb: Founders & Team

> The team behind GRILL X is a tight, ex-Samsung Research deep-tech group led by founder-CEO Jeong Hyun-ki (Steve Jung), built on the conviction that "cooking is essentially science."

Beyond Honeycomb (비욘드허니컴) was founded in 2020 by former Samsung Electronics / Samsung Research engineers specializing in AI, robotics, sensors, and food science. The company is small and deliberately specialist-heavy — roughly 16-18 people, about half of them engineers — and it recruits aggressively for a tiny number of high-caliber hires. This document covers the founder's background and philosophy, the founding story, the leadership team, team composition and culture, and the company's hiring posture.

## Founder & CEO: Jeong Hyun-ki (Steve Jung / 정현기)

- The founder and CEO is **Jeong Hyun-ki**, romanized variously as **Hyungki Jung**, **Jeong Hyeon-gi**, and — the name he uses publicly in English — **Steve Jung** [1][2][7].
- Before founding the company, he spent about **10 years at Samsung Electronics / Samsung Research** (LinkedIn lists Research Staff, Jul 2010 - Jul 2020), where his work focused on **system architecture and innovative research in sensors and food science** [3]. Korean coverage describes him as a **lead ("책임") researcher in Samsung Research's "Think Tank" team**, and the company notes he was the **youngest research leader** there, trained to build innovative technology fast with teams of just 3-4 people [5][6].
- His most visible Samsung-era project is **Entrim 4D**, a VR motion accessory using Galvanic Vestibular Stimulation to let users physically feel on-screen movement. It was unveiled at **SXSW 2016** as a Samsung C-Lab (Creative Lab) project, and Samsung's own newsroom names **"Steve Jung, Creative Leader of the project"** — a direct primary-source link between the CEO and his Samsung research credentials [4][3]. (Note: several Korean articles loosely attach "2016" to his departure; this almost certainly refers to the Entrim 4D launch year. His Samsung tenure on LinkedIn runs to mid-2020, matching the company's 2020 founding [3][7].)
- His foundational philosophy is that **"cooking is essentially science" (요리는 과학)**: he argued that taste, traditionally left to a chef's hands and intuition, can be quantified — and that precisely because it is so intuition-driven, it is ripe for technological disruption [1][8]. He framed the long-term vision as turning analog commercial kitchens into a **digital platform** ("레스토랑의 디지털 플랫폼화") and an ecosystem where chefs' flavors are digitized [5][9].

## Founding Story & Motivation

- The idea germinated during his Samsung Research tenure, where he had already spent years thinking about and researching food science [10][5].
- After the Entrim 4D work, he left Samsung **with several colleagues** to found Beyond Honeycomb in **2020**, betting that (a) AI applied to robotics could make cooking "scientific" and repeatable, and (b) rising labor costs and worsening kitchen staffing shortages would drive restaurant demand for cooking robots [2][8].
- He has credited early trust as decisive: NAVER had reached out during the Entrim 4D period, and the relationship led to NAVER D2SF backing the company on the strength of the idea and the team's proven technical credibility [5].
- Because each cooking session yields only a single data point — and variables like cut, thickness, and seasoning complicate collection — the team initially concentrated on common proteins (steak, chicken, salmon, then pork belly) and ran tens of thousands of cooking tests to build its dataset [1][11].
- A recurring point of founder pride: **"We're a company of Samsung Research alumni, so we did everything ourselves — from designing the AI model to assembling the robot."** The team built a lightweight AI model that can learn from only a few hundred data points, then built the GRILL X robot to physically execute the recipes the AI found [8].

## Leadership Team & Key Executives

The company's public leadership roster (grillx.ai "About Us") lists nine people; LinkedIn fills in backgrounds [2][3]:

- **Steve Jung — CEO / founder.** Ex-Samsung Research (sensors, food science); creator of Entrim 4D [2][3][4].
- **Zoey Jung — CTO** (as listed on the company site) [2]. LinkedIn separately shows **Da Woon Jung, Co-Founder & CTO**, ex-Samsung Electronics software engineer (2017-2020) with a **Biosystems Engineering** degree from **Seoul National University** — joined Jan 2020 [3]. The public "Zoey Jung" and LinkedIn "Da Woon Jung" appear to be the same person (English nickname vs. given name); treat the CTO identity as the same individual pending direct confirmation.
- **Andrew Kim — CRO.** Ex-Samsung Research "Think Tank" team, mechanical/mechatronics prototype engineer; also the named recruiting contact (andrew.kim@beyondhoneycomb.com) [2][3].
- **Wayne Lee — CFO** [2].
- **Wyatt Kim (Namyun Kim) — Productization / "Robotization Leader."** Joined 2022/2023; previously a mechanical design engineer at Samsung Healthcare (ultrasound systems) for ~7 years [2][3].
- **Mark Kim, Daniel Yang — Robotics Engineers**; **Phill Jang — AI Engineer**; **Jake Huh — Business Strategy** [2].

Multiple co-founders/early staff share the Samsung Research pedigree, and the company explicitly markets the team as including researchers who won the **"Samsung Employee of the Year" award (올해의 삼성인상)** and the **Jang Yeong-sil Award (장영실상)** for AI / robotics work [12].

## Team Composition, Size & Culture

- **Headcount:** about **16 employees** per LinkedIn (founded 2020, Seoul), reported as **+18.8% year-over-year (about +3 people)** [3]. A 2024 recruiting profile lists **18 total / 9 developers** [5]. So the company is roughly **16-18 people, about half engineers** — small and specialist-dense rather than broad.
- **Composition:** the founding and core team is built from **ex-Samsung Research/Electronics AI, robotics, sensor and food-science researchers**, augmented by graduates of **Seoul National University** and other top institutions; the company describes itself as targeting **"high talent density"** (높은 인재 밀도) with a team of domain specialists [12][3]. It vertically integrates sensor + AI + robotics, in-housing every core component except the motors [3][2].
- **Culture (self-described):**
  - "**Beyond Hype**" — *"We don't chase trending technologies. We build what truly changes the world"* — positioning itself as building the substance behind the AI/robotics hype rather than riding it [13].
  - **Agile, "fail fast" mindset** — bias toward rapid attempts and lessons-learned over seeking a perfect approach up front [5].
  - **Ambitious, "think extraordinarily" goal-setting** — deliberately uncomfortable targets (the recruiting deck cites stretch goals like an 800B-won valuation by 2026 and a 10-trillion-won valuation by 2028) [5].
  - **Compressed, high-focus work hours:** an **11am-6pm, ~35-hour week** (lunch included), framed as letting strong people work intensively and rest fully [5].

## Hiring & Recruiting Posture ("인재 영입 / Join Us")

- The company runs an active **"인재 영입" (Talent Acquisition)** section and posts openings on its own site plus Wanted, Linkareer, and similar boards. Open roles cluster around **Robotics/Mechanical Engineers, SW Engineers, AI Engineers, and master's/PhD researchers**, with summer engineering internships (Robotics + AI) as well [14][13][15][16].
- Recruiting copy leans hard on the **elite-team narrative**: it touts Samsung Research alumni who won the Samsung Employee of the Year and Jang Yeong-sil awards, SNU and other top-tier graduates, and pitches candidates to *"design change rather than have your talent consumed"* [12][13].
- **Compensation** is positioned as **"better than big-company top-tier"** — a quoted band of about **KRW 60M (new grad) to KRW 140M (experienced)** plus performance-based raises and **stock options**; interns are paid ~KRW 2.5M/month with strong preference for full-time conversion [12][16].
- **Process:** résumé/portfolio by email → 1st interview (skills + culture fit) → 2nd interview → reference check → offer, with a 3-month probation for full-time hires [12].
- Engagement extends to the talent pipeline beyond hiring: the company has hosted groups of **M.Sc. Robotics students from TU Delft** at its Seoul HQ and run live GRILL X demos in working K-BBQ restaurants for them [17].
