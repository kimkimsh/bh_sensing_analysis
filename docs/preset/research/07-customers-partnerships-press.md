# Beyond Honeycomb: Customers, Partnerships & Press

> How Beyond Honeycomb (비욘드허니컴) grew its GRILL X AI cooking robot from a CES 2022 debut to 100-plus deployed customers across corporate cafeterias, a 5-star hotel, premium-pork franchises and small restaurants — and how it is now stepping into North America.

Beyond Honeycomb, a Seoul food-tech startup founded in 2020 by former Samsung Research AI and robotics engineers, sells its flagship GRILL X AI grilling robot to a deliberately broad customer base: from neighborhood eateries to major-corporate cafeterias and a luxury hotel. This document maps the company's customers, named partnerships, public events, recognition and global-expansion strategy, drawing on company releases and Korean and English press from 2022 through late 2025.

## Customers & Deployments

- **Customer milestone — 100+ accounts.** On 6 November 2025 the company announced its cumulative customer count had crossed 100 brands/sites, a figure spanning aged-pork franchises, small neighborhood restaurants, large-corporate cafeterias and concession sites, and 5-star hotels [1]. A company recruiting page earlier in 2025 cited "60+ customer brands within one year of GRILL X launch," consistent with rapid growth toward the 100 figure [2].

- **NAVER corporate cafeterias (proof-of-concept).** Beyond Honeycomb's early backer NAVER (via accelerator D2SF) hosted the startup in NAVER's second HQ building, and its cooking solution was deployed in NAVER's corporate cafeterias, which the company repeatedly cites as the proof that the system delivers both speed and quality at scale [3][4]. Korean coverage notes the technology was "already validated at NAVER's second HQ" before the Hanam Pig deal [5].

- **KT cafeterias and concession sites.** Telecom KT is named among the large-enterprise cafeteria/concession customers in the 100-customer portfolio [1]. KT is also Beyond Honeycomb's co-development partner for the hotel deployment below.

- **Andaz Seoul Gangnam (Korea's first 5-star-hotel AI chef robot).** The Hyatt luxury-lifestyle hotel Andaz Seoul Gangnam introduced an "AI Chef Grill Robot" in May 2023 — described as the first cooking robot adopted by a hotel in Korea [6][7]. The robot, later nicknamed "ANDI," was jointly developed for the hotel by Beyond Honeycomb and KT [6][8]. It reproduces the steak recipe of Michelin-star-trained executive chef Damien Selme by using molecular sensors to analyze Maillard reaction and juice-retention factors; the signature debut dish was the "Open StAIk Sandwich" (KRW 38,000) [7]. In June 2024 the hotel extended the robot to a three-item "ANDI's Salad" line [8]. By late 2025 Andaz was cited as the 5-star hotel that adopted Korea's first AI chef robot [1].

- **Premium-pork franchise — Hanam Pig (see Partnerships).** The aged/premium pork-belly segment is explicitly part of the customer base [1].

- **Small businesses via government vouchers.** Supported by Korean government voucher programs, Beyond Honeycomb made its robots affordable for small merchants, positioning itself as a driver of AI adoption among small foodservice operators [3][4].

## Named Partnerships

- **Hanam Pig (하남돼지집) — MOU, January 2024.** Beyond Honeycomb signed an MOU on 17 January 2024 with the premium pork-belly franchise Hanam Pig to jointly develop, commercialize and globally expand an AI chef / "AI Grill Kitchen" solution [9][10][11]. The robot is aimed at automating Hanam Pig's signature high-temperature 초벌 (first-sear) process, which today requires staff to work 10–15 minutes in a ~500°C searing room [10][12]. Beyond Honeycomb said it would use its dataset of 500,000+ food items to analyze Hanam Pig's cooking style and build a customized solution covering meat-quality maintenance, the searing technique and service method, drawing on Hanam Pig's 13 years of operating data [9][12]. The plan: deploy first at the Pungsan direct-run store, then roll out to franchise locations nationwide and pursue overseas markets together; Korean press reported AI-grilled pork could be served "as early as summer 2024" [12]. The two companies jointly exhibited the AI Grill Kitchen solution at "Boom Up Korea 2024" (COEX, 15–16 April 2024) [13].

- **KT (Andaz robot co-development).** KT, which owns the Andaz Seoul Gangnam property, co-developed the hotel's AI chef grill robot with Beyond Honeycomb [6][8].

- **Nongshim (CES 2022 collaboration).** At CES 2022, Beyond Honeycomb teamed with food giant Nongshim to present a "Jjapaguri (ram-don) Chef Edition" — the dish made famous by the film Parasite — paired with a roast-chicken element from chef Kim Min-ji [14].

- **KCGANA — North America MOU, November 2025.** On 12 November 2025 Beyond Honeycomb signed a strategic MOU with the Korean Cuisine Globalization Association of North America (KCGANA, 북미주 한식세계화 총연합회; head Moon Jun-ho), which represents Korean restaurants across the US and Canada [15][16]. Ahead of signing, 21 association members visited Beyond Honeycomb to watch a GRILL X demonstration. The partnership targets K-BBQ promotion and Korean-restaurant competitiveness through joint demos, promotions and research — the company's most concrete North-America distribution channel to date [15][16].

## Events & Recognition

- **CES 2022, Las Vegas.** Beyond Honeycomb exhibited at booth CP-29 during CES 2022 (5–7 January) [17]. Its AI chef reproduced three Korean master-chef dishes and served over 1,000 dishes in just four hours, drawing long lines at a booth in front of the Las Vegas Convention Center [18][14]. The featured chefs included Kim Min-ji (Mishmash / 미쉬매쉬), Lee Jae-hoon (Cadel Lupo / 까델루뽀) and Shin Seung-hwan (Tereno / 떼레노) [19][14]. Korean reporters and even event security were reportedly won over by the robot-made "honey chicken" [19]. CEO Steve Jung framed the platform as "digitizing the flavors chefs create" [14].

- **Pangyo Techno Valley "Singularity" AI-chef restaurant (2022).** Beyond Honeycomb opened "Singularity," a restaurant where AI chef robots are the main cooks, in Pangyo Techno Valley; the Pangyo Techno Valley YouTube channel featured the robots cooking, and a press release circulated the demonstration internationally in September 2022 [20].

- **Trade-show circuit (2024).** Beyond Honeycomb planned active participation in 2024 food and franchise expos, including MEAT EXPO (SETEC, June) and the Franchise Startup Expo (COEX, August), alongside Boom Up Korea 2024 [13].

- **Awards / honors.** No specific CES Innovation Award or named industry prize for Beyond Honeycomb was confirmed in the sources reviewed; the company's public "recognition" claims center on its CES 2022 draw, investor validation, and the "Korea's first hotel AI chef robot" first-mover distinction rather than a formal award. Treat any specific award claim as unverified. (Confidence: low.)

## Global Expansion Strategy

- **North America first.** From its 2022 CES debut Beyond Honeycomb signaled US entry "by the end of 2022," citing US kitchen-labor shortages as the ideal market for its solution [18][20]. The strategy crystallized with the July 2025 Series A bridge (~KRW 10B / ~US$7.2M), whose proceeds are earmarked for mass-production scale-up plus "distribution partnerships and local testbeds, starting in North America" [3][21].

- **Distribution channel taking shape.** The November 2025 KCGANA MOU gives the company a member network of US/Canada Korean restaurants as an entry beachhead for K-BBQ-focused GRILL X deployment [15][16].

- **Stated ambition.** CEO Jeong Hyun-ki (romanized Hyungki Jung / Jeong Hyeon-gi / "Steve Jung") said the bridge round recognized the company's "technology and global potential," aiming "to become a leader in the global food tech market, starting with North America," and elsewhere set a goal of becoming the global No. 1 in grill automation by accelerating into North America and Europe [3][1]. Company recruiting material lists the US, Japan, Middle East, Europe and Southeast Asia as 2025-and-beyond expansion targets, with a longer-term move into the home-appliance market after 2027 [2].

- Andaz Seoul Gangnam adopted Korea's first hotel AI chef grill robot in May 2023; robot "ANDI," co-developed with KT, reproduces chef Damien Selme's steak; signature "Open StAIk Sandwich" KRW 38,000 [6][7][8].
- Hanam Pig MOU signed 17 January 2024 — joint R&D, commercialization and global expansion of an "AI Grill Kitchen" automating Hanam Pig's ~500°C first-sear process [9][10][12].
- 500,000+ food-item dataset used to analyze Hanam Pig's cooking style; Hanam Pig contributes 13 years of operating data [9][12].
- CES 2022 (Las Vegas, 5–7 Jan, booth CP-29): reproduced 3 Korean master-chef dishes, served 1,000+ dishes in 4 hours [17][18][14].
- CES 2022 featured chefs: Kim Min-ji (Mishmash), Lee Jae-hoon (Cadel Lupo), Shin Seung-hwan (Tereno); CES collaboration with Nongshim on "Jjapaguri Chef Edition" [19][14].

- KCGANA (US/Canada Korean-restaurant association) MOU signed 12 November 2025; 21 member restaurateurs attended a GRILL X demo [15][16].
- North-America-first global plan funded by the July 2025 ~KRW 10B (~US$7.2M) Series A bridge; total funding ~KRW 19.6B (~US$14.1M) [3][21].
