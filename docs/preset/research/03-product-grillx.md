# GRILL X: Beyond Honeycomb's AI Grilling Robot

> GRILL X is Beyond Honeycomb's flagship product, an AI-powered grilling robot that mounts onto a kitchen's existing grill and uses molecular sensors plus computer vision to deliver chef-level, consistent results for pork, beef, and fish at the press of a single button.

## What GRILL X Is

GRILL X (그릴X) is an AI grilling robot built for commercial kitchens by Beyond Honeycomb, a Seoul-based food-tech startup founded in 2020 by former Samsung researchers in AI and robotics [1][7]. It is marketed as "the world's first AI-grilling solution" and "the AI grilling robot delivering chef-level precision — every time" [1][5].

Rather than replacing a kitchen's cooking surface, GRILL X is a robotic arm and sensing module that sits over an existing grill and automates the cooking itself. Its core promise is "chef-level judgment, machine-level consistency" — the same sear, taste, and quality regardless of who is on shift, the time of day, or the location [2].

Key hardware and capability highlights from the company's product page [2]:

- **Grill Arm** — a 5-axis robotic arm for "chef-level finesse, no press, no mess."
- **Simple UI** — a clean, intuitive interface usable by anyone.
- **Easy Integration** — a modular design that fits onto any grill with a minimal footprint.
- **Smart Flare-Up Detection** — detects and filters out harmful flare-ups in real time (the company also describes "flame detection sensors" and "soft-joint actuators" for compact kitchens) [2][6].
- **5+ Patents** and **150+ robots deployed** as of the current product page [2].

## How It Works: Sensing and the 4-Step Flow

GRILL X pairs a patented molecular sensor with an AI engine. The sensor and AI "precisely measure flavor factors like Maillard browning, drip loss, and char level," and the AI then adjusts grill time and grilling patterns in real time based on each protein's condition [1][2]. At the company's 2025 US debut, Beyond Honeycomb described the system as performing real-time molecular sensing that recognizes chemical changes — Maillard reaction, fat rendering, charring levels, and collagen breakdown — reportedly in about 0.3 seconds, enabling autonomous adjustment without human intervention [6][8].

The customer-facing experience is condensed into a four-step, one-button flow ("Grill Like a Pro with One Button") [1][5]:

1. **Press Start** — the operator presses a single button to begin.
2. **AI Cooks Automatically** — fully automated searing and flipping, with no manual handling required.
3. **Analyzed in Real Time** — sensors and AI continuously read the meat's condition.
4. **Perfect Results, Every Time** — consistent, chef-level doneness on every cut.

This maps to the four pillars the company lists under "Chef-Level Precision Made Simple": AI Quality Control Technology, an Automated Grilling System (fully automated searing and flipping), Easy Integration (works with any grill brand or type), and Simple Operation (wireless, single-button control) [1].

## Form Factor, Installation, and Integration

GRILL X is designed as a modular unit that mounts over a kitchen's existing grill, broiler, or griddle "no matter the brand or type" [1][2]. The product imagery shows it engineered to fit alongside leading commercial grillers, emphasizing a minimal footprint [2].

Published unit specifications (from the company FAQ) [3]:

- **Dimensions:** Width 60 cm (23.6 in), Length 100 cm (39.4 in), Height 180.5 cm (71 in).
- **Weight:** 40 kg (88 lbs).
- **Electrical:** Standard 220V outlet (or 110V in the US), max 1.5 kW.
- **Fire/ventilation:** Must be installed under a certified ventilation hood.
- **Connectivity:** Requires an internet connection (for AI updates and operation).
- **Hygiene/certification:** Detachable grill and a wipeable, hygienic body; CE certified for food-safe operation, with NSF and UL certifications listed as in progress [2].

**Installation time is reported inconsistently across sources.** Earlier company messaging and many third-party profiles cite a roughly 10-minute, plug-and-play install with no construction [4][9][10]. The current company FAQ instead states installation takes "less than 30 minutes" [3]. Both figures emphasize the same value proposition: fast deployment without renovation. (A third-party listing also cites a "7.8-inch width," which contradicts the company's official 60 cm / 23.6 in figure and appears to be an error in that listing [9].)

## Throughput, Capacity, and Target Proteins

GRILL X targets pork, beef, and fish as its headline proteins [7]. In practice the company and resellers list a broader range: pork belly, galbi (ribs), ribeye, striploin, short ribs, marinated cuts, chicken, and other proteins per the FAQ [3], with third-party listings extending to lamb, salmon, and eel [9].

Operating cadence per the FAQ [3]:

- **Grilling time:** 2–5 minutes per portion.
- **Capacity per cycle:** 5–6 portions simultaneously.
- **Daily human interaction:** under ~5 minutes per hour of operation.

**Hourly throughput is a contested figure** that appears to have been revised downward over time as the product matured. Reported values span:

- **~144 servings/hour** — cited by data aggregators and earlier company-sourced material [4][10].
- **Up to 120 servings/hour** — third-party robot marketplace listing [9].
- **Up to 80 servings/hour** — the figure used at the official 2025 NRA Show US debut and in trade coverage [6][8].
- **60 servings/hour** — the company's own recent social-media campaign ("built for peak hours, 60 servings per hour") [11].

The most authoritative recent primary statement (the company's NRA Show press release) is "up to 80 servings per hour" [8]. The higher ~144 figure should be treated as an earlier or maximum-capacity claim rather than a current operating spec.

GRILL X does not come bundled with a grill/charbroiler — it integrates with the operator's existing equipment — and operators can use their own meat [3].

## Product Variants and SKUs

The product page presents two distinct hardware configurations, each with its own "Anatomy" spec section [2][3]:

- **GRILL X** — the flagship model that includes the molecular sensor for maximum precision.
- **Zephyr** — a simpler, sensor-free version. The FAQ confirms two robot models: "one model includes a molecular sensor for extra precision, and the other is a simpler sensor-free version" [3]. The product page's "Zephyr Anatomy" section identifies Zephyr as that second SKU [2].

Beyond Honeycomb offers both rental and purchase options, with warranty and ROI terms referenced in the FAQ, and runs an "early access pricing" program for high-volume kitchens and distribution partners [2][3].

## Value Proposition for Operators

GRILL X is positioned around consistency, labor relief, and ease of training [1][2][5]:

- **Consistency at any scale** — "no matter the staff, time, or location," every dish comes out the same during lunch rush or dinner crowd.
- **Reduced training burden** — a hotel operations manager testimonial states GRILL X "cut our training time by 80%"; another operator trained a team in "less than 10 minutes" [1][2].
- **Fewer complaints** — a steakhouse supervisor reports they "stopped getting complaints about overcooked meat after switching to GRILL X" [1].
- **Labor economics** — the partnership page claims operators can "reduce labor costs by $80,000+ per year with 100% grilling consistency"; third-party listings describe GRILL X as replacing 2–4 grill staff [4][9].
- **Ease of use** — a K-BBQ franchise owner calls it "the iPhone of grilling — one button, that's all it takes" [1].

Target segments span steakhouses, Korean BBQ (K-BBQ), fast casual, hotels, and corporate dining — from small restaurants to five-star hotels and franchise chains [1][7]. GRILL X debuted in the US at the 2025 National Restaurant Association Show in Chicago, was developed in collaboration with Michelin-trained chefs, and is already deployed across South Korean five-star hotels, national chains, and premium K-BBQ venues; one named deployment is the Andaz Seoul Gangnam hotel, where a robot chef ("Andi") grills steak for an AI steak sandwich menu [6][8][12].

- Install time: ~10 minutes per earlier/aggregator sources; "less than 30 minutes" per current FAQ (contested) [3][4][9].

- Variants/SKUs: GRILL X (with molecular sensor) and Zephyr (simpler, sensor-free) [2][3].
