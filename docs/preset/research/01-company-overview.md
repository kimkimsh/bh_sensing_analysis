# Beyond Honeycomb: Company Overview & History

> Beyond Honeycomb (비욘드허니컴) is a Seoul-based food-tech startup, founded in 2020 by former Samsung Electronics AI and robotics researchers, that builds AI-driven cooking robots — led by its flagship grilling robot GRILL X — to bring chef-level consistency to commercial kitchens facing labor shortages.

## What Beyond Honeycomb Is

Beyond Honeycomb is a South Korean food technology company specializing in AI-based "cooking robot" systems for commercial kitchens. Its core idea is to turn cooking — traditionally an intuition-driven craft — into a measurable, repeatable, data-driven process. The company uses a patented molecular/spectral sensor together with AI to quantify cooking in real time (the Maillard browning reaction, drip loss/juiciness, char level, fat rendering and collagen breakdown), then drives a robot that grills and flips food autonomously to a target quality score. [1][2][6]

- One-line pitch: an AI grilling robot delivering "chef-level grilling with AI precision — every time." [2]
- Founder/CEO Jeong Hyun-ki (romanized variously as Hyungki Jung, Jeong Hyeon-gi, or "Steve Jung") was inspired during his tenure at Samsung Research, summarizing the company's thesis as "Cooking is essentially science." [1][6]
- Industry: food-tech / kitchen robotics ("physical AI"). Aggregators also classify it under robotics, automation and smart systems. [6][9][13]
- The team is described as founded by researchers with roughly 10 years of experience in robotics and food science from Samsung Electronics' research institute. [8]

## Mission, Vision & the Problem It Solves

Beyond Honeycomb frames its purpose around the global kitchen labor crisis. Its mission page states it exists "to help kitchens succeed in a world where labor shortages, rising costs, and consistency challenges make daily operations harder than ever," empowering chefs and operators with AI-driven cooking systems. Its stated vision is to "make advanced cooking technology as essential as ovens and refrigerators in the modern kitchen." [3]

The company's earlier, broader framing (2022) articulated a consumer-facing mission: "Everyone deserves to experience good food anywhere, anytime at a low price. Technology makes it happen." It described itself as "reshaping commercial kitchens to create a digital platform with a digitized set of chef skills and taste data" — serving casual but chef-quality daily meals (sandwiches, burgers, BBQ grill, salads) at low price. [5]

Problems it targets:

- Labor shortage and the rising cost of kitchen labor in foodservice. [4][5][13]
- Scarcity of skilled/expert chefs and the difficulty of training staff to a consistent standard. [4][5]
- Inconsistent food quality across staff, shifts, and locations — the robot is pitched to deliver "consistency at any scale." [2][3]

Business model: an AI kitchen solution that learns and recreates the menus/taste of professional chefs, paired with a copyright/royalty licensing model — restaurants using the system pay royalties to the human chefs whose recipes the robot replicates. Target segments span steakhouse, Korean BBQ (K-BBQ), fast casual, hotel, and corporate dining, from small restaurants and franchise chains to five-star hotels. [1][6][2]

## How the Technology Works (in brief)

- An original chef demonstrates a dish once; a molecular sensor scans it, and the AI chef learns to reproduce the dish on itself within about 48 hours. [5][14]
- During cooking, spectral/molecular sensors analyze progress in real time and the AI autonomously decides the next cooking step based on target scores; identical ingredients can be cooked differently depending on conditions (cut, thickness, preheat). [1][8]
- The approach trains computer-vision models on high-definition food imagery, then calibrates heat and timing in real time; the company reports a dataset of 500,000+ food items and (in early development) over 10,000 cooking tests on common ingredients like steak, chicken, and salmon. [6][1][14]
- GRILL X integrates with existing grills of any brand, installs without construction, and runs on wireless, single-button operation, with soft-joint actuators and flame-detection sensors for compact commercial kitchens. [2][7]

## Company Timeline (2020–2025)

- **2020** — Beyond Honeycomb founded in Seoul, South Korea, by former Samsung Electronics AI and robotics researchers. [6][9][4]
- **~2021 (early stage)** — Receives initial/seed backing from NAVER's corporate accelerator D2 Startup Factory (NAVER D2SF); also selected for CJ's O!Ventures program (6th cohort). [1][4][10]
- **Jan 2022** — Exhibits its AI chef robot at CES 2022 in Las Vegas (booth CP-29), reproducing 3 Korean master-chef dishes and serving 1,000+ dishes in four hours. [5][11][12]
- **2022** — Moves into NAVER's second HQ building "1784" to serve daily lunch menus to NAVER employees, hardening the solution in a live corporate cafeteria; opens "Singularity," an AI-chef restaurant in Pangyo Techno Valley, and reports ~KRW 1.5B in annual-sales contracts within two months. [10][8]
- **Jul 4, 2023** — Closes a Series A round of ~KRW 7 billion (~US$5.3M), led by LB Investment with POSCO Technology Investment, BA Partners, Korea Development Bank (KDB), and Devsisters Ventures; cumulative funding reaches ~KRW 9.4B. CEO Jeong sets a goal of becoming "Korea's first food-tech decacorn." [10][16][6]
- **Jan 2024** — Signs an MOU with premium pork-belly brand Hanam Pig (하남돼지집) to co-develop and commercialize an AI chef solution and pursue global expansion; total investment to date cited as ~KRW 9.4B. [14]
- **Mar 2024** — Featured as "South Korea's Robotics Innovator Redefining Restaurant Kitchen Dynamics." [15]
- **May 17, 2025** — Unveils and brands its flagship AI grilling robot **GRILL X** at the 2025 National Restaurant Association (NRA) Show in Chicago; developed in collaboration with Michelin-trained chefs. [7][17]
- **Jul 9, 2025** — Announces a Series A bridge round of ~KRW 10 billion (~US$7.2M), led by existing investor LB Investment with KDB, BNK Venture Investment, Devsisters Ventures, and the Korea Technology Finance Corporation (KIBO / Technology Guarantee Fund); cumulative funding reaches ~KRW 19.6B. [4][16][1]
- **Aug 2025** — Andaz Seoul Gangnam (Hyatt) launches new menu items grilled by GRILL X (robot chef "Andi"), including an AI steak sandwich, in its "Bites & Bliss" package — a flagship five-star-hotel deployment. [18][19]
- **Dec 2025** — Reported to be rolling GRILL X out nationwide across Korean kitchens as part of Korea's "physical AI" push, backed by ~US$14M in investment and government voucher support, while preparing for mass production and North American expansion. [13]

- HQ address: Gangnam-gu, Bongeunsa-ro 230, 3F, Seoul, South Korea 06135. [2][3]
- Founder & CEO: Jeong Hyun-ki / Hyungki Jung / "Steve Jung." [1][6][8]
- Leadership also includes Zoey Jung (CTO), Andrew Kim (CRO), Wayne Lee (CFO). [20]

- Flagship product: GRILL X (그릴X) — AI grilling robot for pork, beef, and fish. [1][2][4]
- Throughput: contested — company/2022-era material cites ~144 servings/hour, while 2025 sources (TrendHunter, MLex) state "up to 80 grilled servings per hour." [7][13]
- Installation: integrates with existing grills of any brand; installs in ~10 minutes with no construction. [2][7]
- Dataset: 500,000+ food items; chef dish learned/reproduced within ~48 hours. [6][5]
- Series A (Jul 4, 2023): ~KRW 7B (~US$5.3M); led by LB Investment. [10][6]
- Series A bridge (Jul 9, 2025): ~KRW 10B (~US$7.2M); led by LB Investment. [4][16]
- Cumulative funding: ~KRW 19.6B (~US$14.1M) per Korean press; aggregators report lower figures — CB Insights ~$12.78M over 5 rounds (11 investors), The Company Check ~$12.65M. Figures vary by source and methodology. [4][16][9]
- Contact: contact@beyondhoneycomb.com; sites: beyondhoneycomb.com (KR), beyondhoneycomb.com/eng (EN), grillx.ai (product). [2]

> Note on contested numbers: Cumulative funding totals diverge between Korean primary press (~KRW 19.6B / ~US$14.1M) and Western aggregators (CB Insights ~US$12.78M; The Company Check ~US$12.65M; an earlier LinkedIn-sourced figure of ~US$9.3M).
