# Beyond Honeycomb: Funding History and Investors

> Beyond Honeycomb (비욘드허니컴), the Korean AI-cooking-robot startup behind GRILL X, has raised a reported cumulative KRW 19.6 billion (about US$14.1 million) across a NAVER D2SF seed, a July 2023 Series A, and a July 2025 Series A bridge, with LB Investment as its anchor investor.

## Overview

Beyond Honeycomb is a Seoul-based food-tech company (founded 2020) led by CEO Jeong Hyun-ki (정현기; romanized variously as "Hyungki Jung" or "Jeong Hyeon-gi"). Its funding story has three documented stages: an early seed round from NAVER's corporate accelerator D2 Startup Factory (D2SF) around 2021, a KRW 7 billion Series A announced 4 July 2023, and a KRW 10 billion Series A bridge announced 9 July 2025 [1][3][6].

The company itself reports a cumulative raised total of KRW 19.6 billion (about US$14.1 million) as of the 2025 bridge round [1][2]. Independent data aggregators report lower figures — Tracxn lists US$12.6 million and CB Insights lists US$12.78 million — for reasons explained in the reconciliation section below [7][8].

Funds have been earmarked for advancing GRILL X mass-production systems, strengthening quality-control infrastructure, and preparing for global expansion, with an initial focus on the North American market [1][2][3].

Notes on the table:

- **Seed (2021).** NAVER D2SF announced its investment in Beyond Honeycomb (alongside fellow food-tech startup Robo Arete) on 25 March 2021. The Korean release states the D2SF investment was made as a co-investment with POSTECH Holdings; the exact amount was not disclosed [9]. CB Insights and some aggregators date the founding/seed activity to 2020 and list additional undated early rounds [8].
- **Series A (2023).** The Korean primary releases (Yonhap, VentureSquare, Platum, MoneyToday) uniformly name five participants beyond the lead: LB Investment led, with POSCO Technology Investment (포스코기술투자), BA Partners (비에이파트너스), Korea Development Bank (산업은행), and Devsisters Ventures participating. After this round the company's cumulative total reached KRW 9.4 billion (94억) [3][4][5]. The English-language Chosun write-up names only four of these (it omits BA Partners and renders POSCO Technology Investment as "POSCO Venture Capital") [4].
- **Series A bridge (2025).** Led again by existing backer LB Investment, with KDB, BNK Venture Investment, Devsisters Ventures, and the Technology Guarantee Fund (기술보증기금 / KIBO, the Korea Technology Finance Corporation) participating as new and follow-on investors [1][2][6].

## Investors

Named investors across all rounds:

- **LB Investment** — lead investor in both the 2023 Series A and the 2025 Series A bridge; the company's anchor VC [3][6].
- **Korea Development Bank (KDB / 산업은행)** — participated in both the 2023 Series A and the 2025 bridge [3][6].
- **Devsisters Ventures** — participated in both the 2023 Series A and the 2025 bridge [3][6].
- **POSCO Technology Investment (포스코기술투자)** — participated in the 2023 Series A; this entity is rendered in some English sources as "POSCO Venture Capital" / "POSCO Capital" [3][4].
- **BA Partners (비에이파트너스)** — participated in the 2023 Series A (named consistently in Korean releases; sometimes omitted in English summaries) [3][5].
- **BNK Venture Investment** — new investor in the 2025 Series A bridge [1][6].
- **Technology Guarantee Fund / KIBO (Korea Technology Finance Corporation, 기술보증기금)** — participated in the 2025 bridge [1][6].
- **NAVER D2SF (D2 Startup Factory)** — seed-stage backer (~2021) [9].
- **POSTECH Holdings** — co-investor alongside NAVER D2SF in the 2021 seed [9].

CB Insights states Beyond Honeycomb has 11 investors total; Tracxn lists 10 institutional investors. The named entities above account for the investors disclosed in primary press releases [7][8].

## Reconciling the Conflicting Cumulative Totals

Three different cumulative totals circulate; they can be reconciled as follows.

- **KRW 19.6 billion ≈ US$14.1 million (company / Korean press).** This is the figure Beyond Honeycomb itself reported at the 2025 bridge, and it is the most authoritative for the KRW total [1][2]. The implied exchange rate is roughly 1,390 KRW per USD, consistent with mid-2025 rates. Note an internal arithmetic gap: the company reported KRW 9.4 billion cumulative after the 2023 Series A [3], and KRW 9.4B + KRW 10B = KRW 19.4B, slightly below the stated KRW 19.6B. The ~KRW 0.2 billion difference is most plausibly rounding or a small undisclosed interim amount; sources do not itemize it (confidence: medium).
- **Tracxn: US$12.6 million.** Tracxn counts three funding rounds (1 seed + 2 early-stage) and converts disclosed KRW amounts at historical (round-date) exchange rates rather than a single current rate. Converting the 2023 round at ~1,320 KRW/USD and the 2025 round at ~1,375 KRW/USD, and treating the seed as undisclosed, produces a lower dollar sum than a single blended-rate conversion of the full KRW 19.6B [7].
- **CB Insights: US$12.78 million over 5 rounds.** CB Insights records five rounds (including an incubator/accelerator entry and additional early activity) and reports the 2025 bridge as US$7.28 million on 9 July 2025. Its US$12.78M total reflects only the dollar values it has individually verified, with undisclosed/seed amounts effectively under-counted, and uses round-date FX rather than a current blended rate [8].

In short: the **KRW total (19.6B) is stable across primary sources**; the **USD totals diverge because aggregators (a) apply historical per-round exchange rates instead of one current rate, (b) cannot price the undisclosed seed, and (c) differ on how many rounds to count** (3 vs. 5). The company's own "≈US$14.1M" figure is a single-rate conversion of the full KRW 19.6B and therefore sits above the aggregators' round-by-round sums [1][7][8].

## Use of Funds and Strategy

According to the 2025 bridge announcements, the proceeds are directed to [1][2][6]:

- **Mass production.** Advancing and solidifying the GRILL X mass-production (양산) system to meet rapidly growing demand.
- **Quality-control infrastructure.** Substantially strengthening production capacity and quality-management systems.
- **North America expansion.** Building distribution partnerships and establishing local test beds, beginning with the North American market, as the first phase of a broader global strategy.

CEO Jeong Hyun-ki framed the bridge round as "a key milestone in recognizing our technology and global potential," stating the company aims "to become a leader in the global food-tech market, starting with North America" [1][2]. Earlier, at the 2023 Series A, he set a long-term ambition of becoming "Korea's first food-tech decacorn" (a company valued at KRW 10 trillion) [3][5].

## Key Facts

- Cumulative funding reported by the company: **KRW 19.6 billion (~US$14.1 million)** as of the July 2025 bridge round [1][2].
- Seed: **NAVER D2SF**, announced 25 March 2021, co-invested with **POSTECH Holdings**; amount undisclosed [9].
- Series A: **KRW 7 billion (~US$5.3 million)**, announced **4 July 2023**, led by **LB Investment** with POSCO Technology Investment, BA Partners, KDB, and Devsisters Ventures; cumulative reached **KRW 9.4 billion** afterward [3][4][5].
- Series A bridge: **KRW 10 billion (~US$7.2 million)**, announced **9 July 2025**, led by **LB Investment** with KDB, BNK Venture Investment, Devsisters Ventures, and Technology Guarantee Fund (KIBO) [1][2][6].
- Aggregator totals: **Tracxn US$12.6M (3 rounds)**; **CB Insights US$12.78M (5 rounds, 11 investors)** — both lower than the company's KRW-based ~US$14.1M because of historical-rate conversion and undisclosed-round treatment [7][8].
- CB Insights records the 2025 bridge as **US$7.28 million on 9 July 2025** [8].
- Use of funds: GRILL X mass production, quality-control infrastructure, and North America market entry (distribution partnerships + local test beds) [1][2].
